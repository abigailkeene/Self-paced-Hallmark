Phishing Awareness Training Module – Hallmark

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyberattack where attackers pose as trusted individuals or organizations to trick users into revealing sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, or personal details. These attacks are often delivered through email, text, or fake websites that look legitimate. Phishing can lead to identity theft, data breaches, and financial loss.

Email Phishing

Email phishing is the most common form of phishing, where attackers send emails that appear to be from legitimate sources such as banks, coworkers, or service providers. These messages often contain urgent language, suspicious links, or attachments that prompt users to take immediate action.

Spear Phishing

Spear phishing is a more targeted form of phishing that uses personal details to make the message seem legitimate.

Smishing and Vishing

Smishing uses text messages (SMS) to trick recipients into clicking malicious links, downloading attachments, or revealing sensitive information. Vishing occurs over voice calls, where attackers pretend to be tech support, banks, or government officials.

Emerging Threats: AI-Powered and Deepfake Phishing

AI-generated phishing uses machine learning to create more realistic and personalized messages. Deepfake phishing takes this further by using synthetic audio or video to impersonate real individuals, such as a CEO or coworker.

Warning: If a message or video seems “off,” trust your instincts and verify the sender using a known contact method before reacting.

Common Red Flags of Phishing

  • Unfamiliar or misspelled email addresses: e.g., IT-Support@micr0soft.com
  • Unexpected attachments or links
  • Urgent or threatening language
  • Requests for sensitive information
  • Poor grammar or formatting

Tip: Always check the sender's email address and avoid clicking on unexpected links. Hover over links before clicking to preview where they lead!

How to Report Emails Impersonating Hallmark

If you receive a message that appears to be from Hallmark and asks for personal information or money, treat it as a scam.

Hallmark will NOT:

  • Ask for payment through third-party services (e.g., Western Union).
  • Promise cash prizes via email.
  • Ask you to download software or files from an email.

Report the scam

  • Use your email client’s tools: Click Report Phishing / Report Spam in Outlook, Gmail, or Apple Mail.
  • Forward to APWG: Send the message to reportphishing@apwg.org.
  • Report to the FTC: File a report at ReportFraud.ftc.gov.

⚠️ Important: Do not click links or download attachments in suspicious messages.
